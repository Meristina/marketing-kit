## Strategic verdict
Marketing is **re-converging on evidence-based brand science**, not abandoning the
classics. The dominant directive is the **brand-building (long) vs activation (short)
balance**, anchored by two canons that are in **open dispute** — which we model as a
*feature* (a soldier + a counter-soldier), not a settled fact.

## Verified findings (high confidence unless noted)
### Brand science (the two canons, in tension)
- **Binet & Field** (IPA, *The Long and the Short of It*, Nov 2013; ~996 IPA Databank
  campaigns): balance long-term brand-building & short-term activation; **~60:40
  brand:activation** for B2C (sweet spot ~62:38); **emotional campaigns ~2× more likely**
  to drive large long-term profit growth. *Not an iron rule.*
  — https://ipa.co.uk/knowledge/effectiveness-research-analysis/les-binet-peter-field
- **Ehrenberg-Bass / Byron Sharp** (*How Brands Grow*): brands grow mainly through
  **penetration** (more buyers), not loyalty — requires **mental availability** (≠ top-of-
  mind awareness; breadth/depth of brand memories via distinctive assets + category entry
  points) **+ physical availability**. ~82% of growth from penetration vs ~2% loyalty.
  — https://marketingscience.info/news-and-insights/how-do-you-measure-how-brands-grow
- **The dispute (model both):** Sharp publicly rejects 60:40 as "not a scientific law"
  (derived from award submissions); Field/Ritson/Hurman push back. Encode both + surface it.
  — https://marketingscience.info/news-and-insights/prof-byron-sharp-skewers-binet-tells-marketers-to-sack-agencies-preaching-share-of-voice

### Customer & strategy
- **JTBD** (Christensen et al., HBR Sept 2016) — canonical customer-needs/innovation
  framework: organize around the customer's "job," not features/demographics. *Cite as
  canonical reference; origins = Ulwick ODI 2002 / Levitt predate it.*
  — https://hbr.org/2016/09/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done
- **B2B 95-5 rule** (Ehrenberg-Bass / LinkedIn B2B Institute): only ~5% of buyers are
  in-market at a time → brand must build future memories for the 95%. *(95-5 is about
  in-market timing, NOT a 95:5 budget split — that was refuted.)*

### Budget splits (GAP 3 — contested, treat as adjustable defaults)
- **B2C ~60:40**, **B2B ~50/50** (customer-facing rounding of empirical **46:54**),
  set via **ESOV** (Share of Voice > Share of Market → tends to grow), calibrated by MMM,
  adjusted by growth stage. Both the rule AND Sharp's critique are live.

### Measurement (GAP 2 — best resolved): a triangulation stack
- **Incrementality / geo-lift RCTs = causal source of truth** & validation layer.
- **MMM (Google Meridian, open-source, Bayesian — calibrated by experiments as priors) =
  privacy-resilient portfolio allocator.**
- **Platform last-touch / MTA = systematically biased tactical diagnostic** (never alone).
- Evidence: Gordon, Moakler & Zettelmeyer, "Close Enough?", *Marketing Science* 2023
  (663 Facebook RCTs) — observational/matching methods **overstate lift ~5-13×**; 69% of
  practitioners distrust last-touch.
  — https://arxiv.org/pdf/2201.07055 · https://blog.google/products/ads-commerce/meridian-marketing-mix-model-open-to-everyone/ · https://www.forrester.com/blogs/is-googles-meridian-the-right-open-source-mmm-solution-for-you/

### Lifecycle / channels (GAP 4 — partial)
- **CDP / first-party data**: McKinsey closed-loop architecture (*durable but pre-2023 —
  flag as dated*).
- **Creator/influencer = long-term brand builder** (IPA 2025 first cross-sector database:
  long-term ROI multiplier **3.35**, highest of all channels; short-term index only 99) →
  belongs in **Brand-Building**, not pure performance.
- **RevOps / sales-marketing alignment**: no strong verified claim — include on judgment.

### AI in marketing
- Mainstream workflow layer: **88% org AI adoption (2025), gen-AI 33→71→79%**, marketing &
  sales a top function for AI revenue; but **only 7% "fully scaled"** → frontier, not turnkey.
  Agentic AI "up to two-thirds of marketing activities" is a *forecast*.
  — https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

  equity/architecture, 4P/7P (as a planning checklist).
- **Reframed / superseded:** AIDA → non-linear full-funnel; loyalty-led growth → penetration-
  led; last-click/MTA → MMM + incrementality.
