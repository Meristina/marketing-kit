# Skill — Segmentation & Personas

Cut the market into groups that behave differently enough to be worth treating
differently, then make the priority groups concrete as **personas**. Good segmentation
is **actionable** (you can reach & serve the group), **substantial** (big enough to
matter), **differentiable** (groups respond differently), and **stable** enough to plan on.

> **Doctrine — stay in lane.** This soldier *describes & prioritises* segments and builds
> personas. It does **not** make the strategic targeting decision or write positioning —
> that is **STP** (Officer 2), which consumes this output as its "S". Prefer **needs / JTBD
> / behavioural** bases over pure demographics (demographics describe, they rarely explain).
> **Never invent** a segment size, share, or stat — research it and cite it; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Choose the segmentation base(s)** fit for the brief — usually a blend:
   - **Needs / JTBD** (the job they're hiring for) — most explanatory; reuse the JTBD output.
   - **Behavioural** (usage, frequency, occasion, loyalty, buying stage).
   - **Attitudinal / psychographic** (values, motivations, risk appetite).
   - **Firmographic** (B2B: industry, size, maturity, buying-group roles) / **Demographic**
     (B2C: age, income, geography) — use as *descriptors*, rarely as the primary cut.
2. **Build the segment map (MECE):** name 3–6 segments that are mutually exclusive and
   collectively exhaustive; for each give the defining cut, the core job/need, and
   observable signals. Ground every size/share figure in research (sales, surveys, market
   reports, search/social) — label estimates `[estimate — verify]`.
3. **Prioritise** segments on **attractiveness** (size, growth, willingness-to-pay, fit
   with the brand) × **ability to win** (reachability, competitive intensity, current
   strength). Rank; mark which to take to STP. Do **not** finalise the target choice here.
4. **Personify the top 1–3 segments** — one persona each, evidence-led, no clichés:
   - Identity & context (who/where/role); the **primary JTBD** + emotional/social layer;
     triggers & CEPs they enter from; buying behaviour & decision criteria; objections/
     anxieties; where they are reachable (channels/media); a real verbatim if sourced.
   - For B2B: map the **buying group** (economic buyer, champion, user, blocker), not one
     person.
5. **Hand off:** the ranked segment map + personas feed **STP** (Officer 2 targeting &
   positioning), CEP work, and downstream channel/message choices.

## Output format
```
SEGMENTATION & PERSONAS — <product / market>
Context detected: <B2B/B2C · market · stage>   |  Base(s) used: <needs/behavioural/…>

SEGMENT MAP (MECE)
  | Segment        | Defining cut       | Core job/need | Size/share | Signals |
  | <name>         | <base>             | …             | …[src]     | …       |
  …

PRIORITISATION (attractiveness × ability-to-win) — ranked, NOT the final target choice
  1. <segment> — why high priority, what would make it the target (defer to STP)
  2. …

PERSONAS (top 1–3 segments)
  ▸ <Persona name> — <segment>
    Context: …   Primary JTBD: …   Triggers/CEPs: …   Decision criteria: …
    Objections/anxieties: …   Reachable via: …   Verbatim: "…" [src or omit]
  (B2B: buying-group roles instead of one persona.)

HANDOFF TO STP (Officer 2)
  - Candidate target(s) + the trade-off the targeting decision must resolve.

SOURCES (every size/share/stat cited; nothing invented)
  - <market/survey/sales sources researched live>
```
