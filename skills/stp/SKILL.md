# Skill — STP (Segmentation · Targeting · Positioning)

The decision spine of strategy: which buyers you will (and won't) serve, and the place you
want to own in their mind. STP turns Officer 1's research into a committed strategic choice.

> **Doctrine — STP owns the CHOICE.** The `persona-segmentation` soldier *describes and
> ranks* segments (the "S"); **STP decides** the target and frames the position. Keep the
> labour split: reuse that segment map, don't rebuild it. Hand the detailed positioning
> statement (Ries&Trout / Dunford craft) to the **positioning** soldier — STP sets the
> anchor, positioning sharpens it. **Never invent** a segment share, growth rate, or
> competitive fit — source it or label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **S — Segmentation (reuse, don't rebuild).** Take the segment map + personas from
   `persona-segmentation`. If absent, request it / run it first. Confirm the bases are
   needs/JTBD-led and the segments are MECE and actionable.
2. **T — Targeting (the decision).** Score candidate segments on **attractiveness** (size,
   growth, WTP, strategic fit, low competitive intensity) × **ability to win** (reach,
   brand strength, capability, defensibility). Choose a targeting **pattern** and justify:
   - **Concentrated** (one segment — focus, fastest to win, fragile),
   - **Differentiated** (several, tailored),
   - **Undifferentiated / mass** (broad reach — note the Ehrenberg-Bass case that brands
     grow by reaching ALL category buyers, not narrowing; surface this tension explicitly),
   - **Niche**. State who you are NOT serving and why — a target is a choice, not a wish.
3. **P — Positioning anchor.** Set the four anchors that the positioning soldier will
   sharpen:
   - **Target** — for whom (the chosen segment + its JTBD/CEPs).
   - **Frame of reference** — the category/alternative you compete against in their mind.
   - **Point of difference** — the one thing you own that matters to them and they can't
     easily get elsewhere (relevant + distinctive + credible).
   - **Reason to believe** — the proof that makes the difference credible.
4. **Tension check (mandatory):** reconcile the *focus* logic of targeting with the
   *penetration* logic of Ehrenberg-Bass (reach all buyers). Say how this brand resolves it
   (e.g. focus the message/CEP, broaden the reach) — don't hide the disagreement.
5. **Hand off:** the positioning anchor → `positioning` soldier (sharpen the statement);
   the target + JTBD/CEPs → brand-building (O3) and demand (O4).

## Output format
```
STP — <product / market>
Context detected: <B2B/B2C · market · stage>

S — SEGMENTS (from persona-segmentation; reused, not rebuilt)
  Map recap: <segments + the ranking already done>   |  source: persona-segmentation

T — TARGETING (the decision)
  | Segment | Attractiveness | Ability-to-win | Verdict |
  Chosen target(s): …   Pattern: <concentrated/differentiated/mass/niche> — why.
  NOT serving: … — why.
  Penetration tension: <how focus vs reach-all-buyers is resolved here>.

P — POSITIONING ANCHOR (for the positioning soldier to sharpen)
  Target:               …
  Frame of reference:   …
  Point of difference:  … (relevant · distinctive · credible)
  Reason to believe:    …

HANDOFF
  → positioning soldier (sharpen statement) · → O3 brand-building · → O4 demand.

SOURCES (every share/rate/fit figure cited; nothing invented)
  - <market/competitor sources researched live>  · persona-segmentation (the segment map).
```
