# Skill — Budget Balance (brand:activation split + ESOV)

Decide **how much goes to long-term brand-building vs short-term activation**, and **how much
total** (via ESOV), as a calibrated, stage-aware recommendation. This is where the marketing
army's central evidence dispute is **resolved out loud**, not hidden.

> **Doctrine — encode BOTH sides, then resolve (the built-in counter-soldier).** This skill is
> deliberately two-headed:
> - **The Binet&Field / ESOV case:** ~**60:40** brand:activation for B2C (B2B ~**50/50**, the
>   customer-facing rounding of empirical **46:54**); **Excess SOV** (SOV > SOM) tends to grow
>   share; emotional brand-building drives long-term profit.
> - **The Ehrenberg-Bass / Sharp counter-critique (run it explicitly):** Sharp publicly rejects
>   60:40 as **"not a scientific law"** (derived from award submissions, survivorship-biased);
>   growth comes from **penetration via mental + physical availability**, not SOV games; the split
>   is a heuristic, not a constant.
> Present **both**, then give a **reasoned, hedged recommendation** for THIS brand — never pick a
> side silently. **The split is a contested DEFAULT, not a law.** Use the **hedged** ESOV
> correlation; **do NOT** claim "SOV is THE driver of growth" (refuted). **Never invent** a SOV%,
> spend figure, or ROI — research/derive it (and calibrate with MMM/incrementality) or label
> `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Frame:** the objective (grow vs defend vs launch), stage, category, and the baseline split +
   SOM/SOV (from O1 and the esov-sov soldier). Name the decision the recommendation drives.
2. **State the 60:40 / ESOV case:** the default split for the context (B2C ~60:40, B2B ~50/50),
   the ESOV logic (SOV above SOM → tends to grow), and why long-term brand matters — cited.
3. **Run the Sharp counter-critique (mandatory):** argue the other side honestly — 60:40 is not a
   law, survivorship bias in the source, penetration/availability over SOV, creative quality and
   category dynamics dominate. Don't strawman it.
4. **Calibrate, don't assert:** adjust the default by **MMM** (saturation/mROI per side),
   **incrementality** (what actually moved), growth stage (startups/launch skew shorter-term;
   defending leaders skew longer), margin, and category. The default is a starting point, not the answer.
5. **Resolve transparently:** give a **recommended split as a range with the reasoning and the
   residual uncertainty stated** — what would change it, and how to test it (geo split → O6). Make
   the trade-off legible to the human.
6. **Size total via ESOV (hedged):** translate the share-of-voice ambition into a budget direction
   as a range, flagging the wide variance; defer the channel-level weight to esov-sov.
7. **Hand off:** the causal check ↔ incrementality; the allocation model ↔ mmm; the brand-side
   weight ↔ esov-sov (O3); long-term health ↔ brand-tracking; the decision ↔ Commander/Inspector.

## Output format
```
BUDGET BALANCE — <brand>
Context detected: <B2B/B2C · market · stage>   |  Objective: <grow/defend/launch>   |  Baseline split + SOM/SOV: …
⚠️ The split is a CONTESTED DEFAULT, not a law — both the 60:40/ESOV case AND the Sharp critique are modelled below.

THE 60:40 / ESOV CASE (Binet & Field)
  Default split (B2C ~60:40 / B2B ~50/50 ≈46:54) · ESOV logic (SOV>SOM tends to grow) · long-term emotional effect. [cited]

THE SHARP COUNTER-CRITIQUE (run explicitly)
  "Not a scientific law" / survivorship bias · penetration via mental+physical availability over SOV · creative + category dominate.

CALIBRATION (don't assert)
  MMM (mROI/saturation per side) · incrementality (what moved) · stage · margin · category → adjustments.

RECOMMENDATION (hedged, with reasoning)
  Recommended split: <range> for THIS brand because <…>. Residual uncertainty: <…>. How to test: geo split → O6.

ESOV / TOTAL SIZING (hedged)
  SOV ambition → budget direction (RANGE, wide variance). Channel-level weight → esov-sov.

HANDOFF: → incrementality (test) · → mmm (allocation) · → esov-sov (O3 weight) · → brand-tracking · → Commander/Inspector.

SOURCES (every SOV/ROI/split figure cited; nothing invented; ESOV hedged, not "SOV is THE driver")
  - Binet & Field (IPA) · Sharp / Ehrenberg-Bass (the critique) · MMM/incrementality calibration · <live sources>.
```
