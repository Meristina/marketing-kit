# Skill — Share of Voice / Excess SOV (ESOV)

Decide **how loud the brand must be** to hold or grow its position. The planning lever is
**ESOV = SOV − SOM**: brands with **positive ESOV** (advertising share above market share)
**tend** to grow share; brands with negative ESOV tend to decline. ESOV sizes the *media
weight*; it does not, by itself, build the brand.

> **Doctrine — hedged correlation, not a law.** The Binet & Field / Jones ESOV relationship
> is a **correlation with wide variance** (the conversion of ESOV-points to share varies by
> category, brand size, and creative quality). **NEVER state "SOV is the primary driver of
> growth" — that is refuted.** Use it as one contributing input, **calibrated by MMM**
> (Officer 6) and modified by creative quality (a strong creative multiplier lowers the SOV
> needed). Surface the **Ehrenberg-Bass / Sharp critique** (penetration & availability over
> SOV games). The **overall 60:40 brand:activation split** is decided by the **budget-balance**
> soldier (Officer 6) — here you size the brand-side media weight. **Never invent** an SOV%,
> spend figure, or ESOV-to-growth conversion — source it or label `[assumption — verify]`;
> unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Define the market & measure SOV/SOM:** the category, geography, and media scope. Get
   **Share of Market** (value or volume) and **Share of Voice** (share of category ad spend /
   impressions) from sourced data. State the basis; flag estimates.
2. **Compute ESOV** = SOV − SOM. Positive = investing for growth; ~zero = holding; negative =
   under-supporting (likely to decline). Note the gap vs each major competitor.
3. **Set the ESOV target by objective & stage:** defend (≈0), grow (positive ESOV sized to the
   share ambition), or launch (high ESOV). Translate the ESOV-point gap into an **implied media
   weight / budget direction** — as a **range with the variance stated**, never a precise
   "X points = Y% share" promise.
4. **Apply modifiers:** creative quality (strong distinctive/emotional creative reduces the SOV
   needed), category dynamics, competitor escalation risk, diminishing returns. Tie to the
   distinctive assets (mental-availability) and emotional platform (emotional-creative).
5. **Calibration & critique (mandatory):** state that MMM (Officer 6) must calibrate the
   spend→outcome curve, and surface the Sharp critique — don't present ESOV as deterministic.
6. **Hand off:** the brand-side weight → media/activation planning (O4 for the short-term half),
   the split decision → budget-balance (O6), the spend→outcome calibration → MMM (O6).

## Output format
```
SHARE OF VOICE / ESOV — <brand / category>
Context detected: <B2B/B2C · market · stage>   |  Market & media scope: …

CURRENT POSITION (sourced)
  Share of Market (SOM): …%   [basis + source]
  Share of Voice  (SOV): …%   [basis + source]
  ESOV = SOV − SOM = …pts     (positive=grow / ~0=hold / negative=decline)
  vs key competitors: …

ESOV TARGET (by objective & stage)
  Objective: <defend / grow / launch>  →  target ESOV: …pts
  Implied media weight / budget direction: <RANGE, with variance stated>
  ⚠️ Hedged: ESOV↔growth is a correlation with wide variance — NOT "SOV drives growth".

MODIFIERS
  Creative quality multiplier · category dynamics · competitor escalation · diminishing returns.

CALIBRATION & CRITIQUE
  MMM (O6) must calibrate spend→outcome.  Sharp critique: <penetration/availability over SOV>.

HANDOFF: → O4 activation weight · → O6 budget-balance (the 60:40 split) · → O6 MMM (calibration).

SOURCES (every SOV/SOM/spend figure cited; nothing invented)
  - Binet & Field / Jones ESOV (hedged correlation) · <category spend / share sources researched live>
```
