# Skill — Brand Architecture & Equity

Two linked questions: **how the brands in a portfolio relate** (architecture) and **what the
brand is worth in the customer's mind beyond the product** (equity). Architecture decisions
should serve equity — clarity for customers, leverage for the business, room to grow.

> **Doctrine — two canonical lenses, used together:**
> - **Keller (CBBE pyramid)** — build equity bottom-up: **Salience** (who are you?) →
>   **Performance / Imagery** (what are you?) → **Judgments / Feelings** (what about you?) →
>   **Resonance** (relationship). Diagnose which rung is weak.
> - **Aaker** — brand-equity **assets** (awareness, associations, perceived quality, loyalty,
>   + proprietary assets) and the **architecture spectrum**: branded house (one master brand,
>   e.g. Google) ↔ sub-brands/endorsed ↔ house of brands (independent, e.g. P&G).
>
> Connect to the rest of the army: equity is the *long-term* asset Officer 3 builds
> (mental availability, distinctive assets) and Officer 6 tracks. **Never invent** an
> equity score, awareness %, or brand-value figure — source it or label `[assumption —
> verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Scope:** single brand or portfolio? Detect B2B/B2C, stage, and the brands/products in
   play. List the current architecture as-is.
2. **Equity diagnosis (Keller × Aaker):** climb the CBBE pyramid — rate each rung
   (salience, performance, imagery, judgments, feelings, resonance) with evidence; map to
   Aaker's assets (awareness, associations, perceived quality, loyalty). Name the weak rung
   and the equity gap. Distinguish **mental availability** (breadth of memories — Officer 1's
   CEPs) from classic awareness; flag where they diverge.
3. **Architecture decision (if a portfolio):** place the brands on the spectrum (branded
   house ↔ endorsed/sub-brands ↔ house of brands). Weigh the trade-offs: **leverage/clarity
   & cost-efficiency** (master brand) vs **targeting/risk-isolation & distinct equity**
   (separate brands). Decide structure, naming, and endorsement; justify against customer
   clarity and business leverage. Flag cannibalisation / dilution / over-stretch risks.
4. **Equity-building priorities:** which assets to build or leverage, which CEPs/associations
   to own (hand to Officer 3), and what NOT to stretch the brand onto.
5. **Hand off:** the equity gaps + distinctive associations brief brand-building (O3); the
   architecture decision frames positioning (with O2's positioning soldier) and naming;
   the equity baseline feeds tracking (O6). Flag gaps for the Inspector.
