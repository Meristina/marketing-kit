# Skill — Brand Narrative & Storytelling

Give the brand a **story worth retelling** — a durable narrative that carries the emotional
platform and the positioning consistently across years, channels, and campaigns. Stories
encode memory and emotion better than feature lists; a strong narrative spine makes every
execution feel like the same brand.

> **Doctrine — narrative serves the strategy, not the reverse.** The story carries the
> positioning (O2) and the emotional territory (emotional-creative); it does not invent a
> new strategy. **Customer is the hero, the brand is the guide** (don't make the brand the
> hero). Keep it consistent with the distinctive assets (mental-availability) so the story
> *and* the look reinforce one brand. **Never invent** a founder fact, heritage claim,
> customer story, or statistic — research/verify it or label `[assumption — verify]`;
> unknown → "unknown". Authenticity matters: a fabricated origin story is a liability.

## Procedure
1. **Anchor:** carry the positioning, the emotional territory, the value prop, and the JTBD
   (functional + emotional + social). The narrative dramatises these — it doesn't replace them.
2. **Find the core truth & belief:** the brand's purpose / point of view / "why we exist" —
   grounded in something real (founder, capability, customer outcome, category tension). One
   belief, defensible and true.
3. **Build the narrative spine (customer-as-hero):**
   - **Hero** — the customer (their identity, desire from the JTBD).
   - **Problem/stakes** — the pain and what's at risk (functional + emotional + social).
   - **Guide** — the brand: empathy + authority (the proof it can help).
   - **Plan / shift** — what the brand offers and the change it enables.
   - **Resolution** — the success/transformation; the new state (gains created).
   Tie the arc to the value prop and the emotional feeling to own.
4. **Story system, not one ad:** the master narrative + how it flexes into sub-stories
   (origin, customer stories, product stories, culture) while staying one story. State the
   recurring motifs, tone, and what stays constant (consistency over the long term).
5. **Truth & substantiation check (mandatory):** every factual story element (founder claim,
   heritage, "first to…", customer outcome, stat) is sourced or flagged as needing
   verification — hand substantiation risks to the Inspector.
6. **Hand off:** the narrative + tone → emotional-creative (execution) and mental-availability
   (asset consistency); the message → activation copy (O4); proof gaps → Inspector.

## Output format
```
BRAND NARRATIVE & STORYTELLING — <brand>
Context detected: <B2B/B2C · market · stage>
Anchored in: positioning · emotional territory · value prop · JTBD

CORE TRUTH & BELIEF
  Purpose / point of view: …   (grounded in <real basis>)   |  one defensible belief.

NARRATIVE SPINE (customer = hero, brand = guide)
  Hero (customer): …        Problem/stakes: … (functional/emotional/social)
  Guide (brand): empathy … + authority/proof …
  Plan / shift: …           Resolution / transformation: …

STORY SYSTEM (one story, many forms)
  Master narrative: …
  Sub-stories: origin · customer · product · culture — how each stays the same story.
  Recurring motifs / tone / constants: …

TRUTH & SUBSTANTIATION CHECK
  | Story element (founder/heritage/claim/outcome/stat) | Sourced? | Verify / risk |
  …  → substantiation risks handed to the Inspector.

HANDOFF: → emotional-creative (execution) · → mental-availability (asset consistency) · → O4 copy.

SOURCES (every founder fact / heritage / stat / customer story cited; nothing invented)
  - <verifiable origin / proof / customer sources researched live>
```
