# Skill — Mental Availability (Distinctive Assets × CEP links)

Mental availability is **how easily a brand comes to mind across buying situations** —
breadth (how many CEPs) and depth (how strongly). It is the Ehrenberg-Bass engine of
**penetration**, which is the main source of growth. This skill builds the two things that
create it: **distinctive assets** (the brand's memory hooks) and the **CEP links** those
assets carry.

> **Doctrine (Ehrenberg-Bass / Romaniuk / Sharp):** grow by reaching MORE category buyers
> across MORE CEPs, not by deepening one segment's loyalty. **Distinctive ≠ differentiated:**
> distinctive assets make the brand *recognisable and recallable* (they needn't signal a
> benefit); differentiation is a separate, weaker lever. **Mental availability ≠ awareness:**
> it is recall *in a buying situation*, linked to CEPs. Reuse Officer 1's `category-entry-points`
> output — don't re-derive the CEPs. **Never invent** a recall %, awareness score, or asset
> attribution figure — source it or label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Pull the priority CEPs** from Officer 1's CEP map (the situations to be available in).
   If absent, request/run `category-entry-points` first.
2. **Audit distinctive assets** the brand owns or could own (logo, colour palette, shape/
   packaging, character/mascot, font, tagline/slogan, sonic/jingle, spokesperson, style).
   Score each on the **Distinctive Asset Grid** (Romaniuk):
   - **Fame** — % of category buyers who link the asset to the brand (recognition).
   - **Uniqueness** — % who link it *only* to this brand (not competitors).
   Classify: **invest** (high fame + high uniqueness — protect & use everywhere), **build**
   (uniqueness but low fame), **avoid/fix** (fame but low uniqueness — confusable), **ignore**.
3. **Map assets → CEPs:** for each priority CEP, which distinctive asset(s) trigger recall in
   that situation, and where the link is missing/weak. The goal: each priority CEP has a
   strong, consistent asset cue.
4. **Build plan:** which assets to create/strengthen, which to retire, and the **consistency
   & distinctiveness rules** (use the same assets relentlessly across touchpoints; codify in
   a brand asset guide). Note the penetration logic: broad reach + consistent assets.
5. **Tension note (mandatory):** surface the Binet&Field ↔ Sharp dispute where relevant
   (emotional/long-term effects vs pure availability) — model both, don't hide it.
6. **Hand off:** asset + CEP-link plan → emotional/creative (same officer), media reach &
   ESOV (esov-sov soldier), and consistency rules for demand/activation (O4). Equity baseline
   ties to O2's brand-equity diagnosis; tracking to O6 (brand-tracking/penetration).
