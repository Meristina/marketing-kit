# Skill — Emotional Creative Strategy

Decide *how the brand should make people feel* so it builds memories and demand for the
future — the long-term half of effectiveness. Long-term growth comes mostly from **emotional,
fame-building** creative that primes broad memories; short-term activation leans rational and
is Officer 4's job. This skill sets the **emotional territory and creative platform**, not the
finished ad.

> **Doctrine (Binet & Field, IPA *The Long and the Short of It*, 2013):** emotional campaigns
> are **~2× more likely** to drive large long-term profit growth; the long-term/brand and
> short-term/activation split is **~60:40 for B2C** (sweet spot ~62:38). **State it as a
> research finding with its caveat — "not an iron rule"** — and surface the Ehrenberg-Bass /
> Sharp critique (availability over emotion; 60:40 disputed). **Never invent** an effectiveness
> multiple, an emotion-score, or a benchmark — source it (cite Binet & Field for the 2×/60:40)
> or label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Anchor in strategy:** carry the positioning, the value prop, the jobs (functional +
   **emotional + social**), and the brand-equity feelings gap (O2) and CEPs (O1). Emotion must
   ladder from a real job/feeling, not be decoration.
2. **Choose the emotional territory:** the core feeling the brand should own (e.g. reassurance,
   belonging, status, delight, relief) — tied to the emotional/social layer of the JTBD and
   distinct from competitors' territories. One primary feeling, not five.
3. **Define the creative platform:** the durable creative idea/organising thought that can run
   for years across executions (not a one-off campaign). State what makes it **distinctive,
   emotionally resonant, and consistent** with the brand's distinctive assets.
4. **Fame & feeling devices:** how the creative builds **fame** (talkability, cultural pull,
   broad reach) and **emotional encoding** (story, characters, music, humour, drama) — the
   mechanisms behind the long-term effect. Note the role of consistency over time.
5. **Balance check (mandatory):** position this within the **60:40** brand:activation balance
   (B2C ~60:40, B2B ~50/50) — say how the emotional/long work coexists with O4's activation,
   and surface the Binet&Field ↔ Sharp tension. Don't oversell emotion as the only lever.
6. **Brief & test:** turn the platform into a creative brief (the feeling, the role for the
   brand, the distinctive assets to carry, what to avoid) and the signals to pre-test
   (emotional response, brand attribution, distinctiveness).
7. **Hand off:** asset/consistency rules ↔ mental-availability; reach/weight ↔ esov-sov;
   story craft ↔ narrative-story; short-term conversion creative ↔ Officer 4; long-term
   effect measurement ↔ Officer 6 (brand tracking).
