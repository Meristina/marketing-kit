# Skill — Brand Tracking & Penetration

Measure **brand health over time** so you can tell whether the brand-building is actually working
— and orient the tracker to the metrics that **predict growth** (penetration, mental availability)
rather than the comfortable vanity metrics (raw awareness, claimed loyalty). This is the long-term
counterpart to the short-term effectiveness stack, and it closes the loop with Officer 3.

> **Doctrine — track what predicts growth (Ehrenberg-Bass).** Growth comes mostly from
> **penetration** (more category buyers), enabled by **mental + physical availability**. So the
> tracker centres on: **penetration** (and its trend vs the market), **mental availability** (CEP
> coverage, distinctive-asset fame × uniqueness — measured, not asserted), and **physical
> availability** — with awareness/consideration as context, and **loyalty/NPS read through
> double-jeopardy** (big brands look "more loyal" mostly because they're bigger — don't mistake a
> loyalty bump for a growth lever). **Tracking is correlational/longitudinal — not causal;** pair it
> with incrementality for cause. **Never invent** a tracking score, penetration %, or recognition
> rate — it must come from real research/survey; label estimates `[assumption — verify]`; unknown →
> "unknown". Flag sample/method validity (panel quality, n, weighting) for the Inspector.

## Procedure
1. **Frame & baseline:** the brand-health question and the decision it informs; pull the baseline
   (O1) and the brand-building goals (O3 — the CEPs and distinctive assets to be tracked).
2. **Define the metric set (growth-predictive first):**
   - **Penetration** — % of category buyers who bought you (period); trend vs market.
   - **Mental availability** — CEP coverage/share, distinctive-asset **fame × uniqueness**
     (Romaniuk), salience in buying situations (not just top-of-mind awareness).
   - **Physical availability** — distribution/availability where/when buyers shop.
   - **Context metrics** — awareness, consideration, brand image/associations, NPS — read with
     double-jeopardy in mind, never as the headline.
3. **Design the tracking system:** continuous vs wave, sample frame & size, representativeness,
   question design (avoid leading/recall bias), competitive set, and benchmarks. State the method's
   limits; a biased panel invalidates the read.
4. **Read trends honestly:** movement vs the market and vs benchmarks, with significance/error
   bands — not single-point swings. Separate signal from sampling noise; watch seasonality and
   method changes (don't compare across a methodology break).
5. **Apply the laws as a lens:** does the data fit penetration-led growth and double-jeopardy? A
   "loyalty up, penetration flat" story usually means no real growth. Flag deviations to explain.
6. **Pair with causality:** tracking shows *what* changed; **incrementality/MMM show why** —
   triangulate. Don't claim a tracker movement was caused by a campaign without the causal check.
7. **Close the loop to O3:** feed mental-availability/asset gaps back to brand-building (distinctive
   assets, CEP links, ESOV), and the verdict to the Commander. Flag method validity → Inspector.

TREND READ (honest)
