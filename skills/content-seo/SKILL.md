# Skill — Content & SEO

Earn demand by being **found and useful** when people search — and increasingly when AI answer
engines summarise. Plan content around the customer's **jobs and search intent**, structure it
for topical authority, and make it technically findable. Organic is a compounding,
long-horizon channel; treat traffic as a means to business outcomes, not the goal.

> **Doctrine — intent and usefulness over keyword stuffing.** Modern SEO rewards **genuine
> relevance, experience, expertise, authority, trust (E-E-A-T)** and good UX, not tricks. Map
> content to **real search intent** (informational / commercial / transactional / navigational)
> and to the **JTBD + CEPs**. Account for the shift to **AI/answer engines** (SGE, LLM
> citations) — optimise to be the cited source, not just the blue link. **Never invent** a
> search volume, keyword difficulty, ranking, or traffic figure — pull it from a tool/source or
> label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown". Don't promise rankings (no one controls
> the algorithm) — plan probabilistically.

## Procedure
1. **Goal & baseline:** the business outcome organic should drive (leads, signups, sales,
   authority) and the current organic baseline (traffic, rankings, conversions) from analytics —
   sourced. Define what success means beyond raw sessions.
2. **Intent & keyword research:** map the customer's jobs (JTBD) and CEPs to the queries they
   actually use; classify by intent and funnel stage. Pull volume/difficulty from tools (cite
   them); flag estimates. Include question/long-tail and AI-answer-style queries.
3. **Topic-cluster architecture:** organise into pillar pages + supporting clusters (topical
   authority), with internal linking. Each cluster maps to a job/CEP and a funnel stage — not a
   pile of disconnected posts.
4. **Prioritise:** score opportunities by **search opportunity × ranking difficulty × business
   value** (and existing authority). Sequence quick wins vs long bets; note where paid (full-
   funnel) covers the gap while organic compounds.
5. **Content plan & quality bar:** formats per intent (guide, comparison, tool, case study),
   the E-E-A-T signals (author expertise, first-hand experience, citations, freshness), and how
   each carries the positioning/value prop and distinctive assets. Plan for AI-answer
   citability (clear structure, direct answers, schema).
6. **Technical & on-page health:** crawlability, indexation, site speed/Core Web Vitals, mobile,
   structured data, internal links, canonicalisation — the foundation that lets content rank.
7. **Measure honestly:** organic outcomes (qualified traffic → conversions, assisted via O6),
   rankings/visibility, and share of AI citations — not vanity sessions. Note algorithm-update risk.
8. **Hand off:** on-page conversion ↔ conversion-cro; paid coverage of gaps ↔ full-funnel;
   PLG free-tool/content loops ↔ plg; lead handoff ↔ lead-scoring-lifecycle; causal lift ↔ O6.
