# Skill — Category Entry Points (CEPs)

A **CEP** is a cue a buyer uses to recall brands when a buying situation arises — the
*situational triggers* that bring a category to mind. **Mental availability** = the
breadth (how many CEPs) and depth (how strongly) a brand is linked to these cues. Brands
grow mainly by **penetration**, and penetration grows by being recalled across **more**
CEPs by **more** buyers — not by deepening one segment's loyalty.

> **Doctrine:** ground this in Ehrenberg-Bass / Romaniuk's framework (*How Brands Grow 2*,
> Romaniuk & Sharp). Mental availability ≠ top-of-mind awareness. CEPs are the demand-side
> map; **distinctive assets** (logo, colour, character, sonic) are the supply-side memory
> hooks that link the brand to them. **Never invent** a market figure, a buyer count, or a
> "% of category" — research every external fact and cite it; unknown → "unknown".

## The W-framework (interrogate the buying situation)
Build CEPs by walking the category's buying occasions across these prompts:
- **Why** — the motivation/job behind the purchase (ties to JTBD).
- **When** — time of day / week / season / life-stage trigger.
- **Where** — location or channel the need arises in.
- **While (doing what)** — the concurrent activity.
- **With (whom)** — who they're with / buying for.
- **With what** — co-purchased or paired products.
- **Feeling (how)** — the emotional state driving the occasion.

## Procedure
1. **Define the category & buyer** (from the brief / JTBD output). Note B2B vs B2C — for
   B2B, CEPs are often situational triggers across the buying group + the **95-5** reality
   (only ~5% in-market now → CEPs build future memories for the 95%).
2. **Generate the CEP long-list** by running every W-prompt across real buying occasions.
   Source occasions from research (reviews, forums, search trends, sales data) — not
   imagination. Aim wide first.
3. **Prioritise** each CEP on three lenses (Romaniuk):
   - **Size** — how many category buyers enter via this CEP (how common the occasion).
   - **Share** — how strongly THIS brand is already linked to it (the gap = opportunity).
   - **Relevance/Velocity** — fit with the brand's strengths / momentum of the occasion.
   Rank by Size × (1 − current Share) to find the highest-leverage CEPs to attack.
4. **Audit distinctive assets** the brand owns (fame × uniqueness) that can carry the
   brand–CEP link in memory; flag missing/weak assets.
5. **Hand off:** the priority CEPs + asset gaps brief Officer 3 (mental availability,
   creative, ESOV) and sharpen positioning (Officer 2). Each CEP = a message territory.
