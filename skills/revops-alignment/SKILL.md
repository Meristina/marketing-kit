# Skill — RevOps / Sales-Marketing Alignment

Get marketing, sales, and customer success to operate as **one revenue engine** instead of
three silos handing off (and blaming) across walls. RevOps unifies the **process, data, and
metrics** along the customer's journey so revenue isn't lost in the gaps between teams.

> **Doctrine — best-practice, NOT a proven law.** The evidence base for "alignment → X% more
> revenue" is **thin and often vendor-sourced**; treat such claims with suspicion. Present RevOps
> as **operational best-practice and practitioner judgment**, not a research-backed guarantee. **Do
> NOT cite the popular "aligned companies grow N% faster" stats unless you can verify a credible
> source** — and even then, flag them as weak. The real, defensible value is **fewer handoff
> leaks, shared truth, and faster cycles** — argue it on mechanism, not borrowed statistics.
> **Never invent** an alignment benchmark, win-rate lift, or ROI — research/verify it or label
> `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Map the end-to-end revenue process:** the full journey across marketing → sales → customer
   success (acquisition → lead handoff → opportunity → close → onboarding → retention/expansion).
   Reuse the lifecycle stages + lead scoring (O4) and lifecycle (O5) — don't redefine them.
2. **Shared definitions & a single source of truth:** one agreed definition per stage (MQL/SQL/
   PQL/opportunity/customer), one set of fields, one system of record. Eliminate the
   "marketing's number vs sales' number" problem.
3. **Inter-team SLAs:** the two-way commitments — marketing's lead quality/volume promise, sales'
   response-time/follow-up promise, CS's onboarding handoff — with the consequence/review when
   missed. (Reuses the marketing→sales SLA from lead-scoring-lifecycle.)
4. **Closed-loop data spine:** lead/opportunity disposition flows back to marketing to recalibrate
   targeting and scoring; CS signals flow back to both. Built on the first-party/CDP foundation
   (O5) — one consented data spine, not parallel exports.
5. **Unified metrics & funnel:** shared pipeline/revenue metrics (stage conversion, velocity,
   win rate, CAC, pipeline coverage, NRR) owned across teams, not per-silo vanity numbers. Tie to
   the business objective and the measurement officer (O6).
6. **Operating cadence & ownership:** the rhythm (pipeline reviews, handoff retros, planning) and
   who owns the RevOps function/tooling; the decisions each forum makes. Keep it lightweight —
   process serving revenue, not process for its own sake.
7. **Honest framing & risks:** state where claims are weak; flag change-management reality (this
   is org behaviour, not a tool install), and that tooling alone doesn't create alignment.
8. **Hand off:** lead handoff & scoring ↔ lead-scoring-lifecycle (O4); lifecycle program ↔
   lifecycle-crm-email; data spine ↔ first-party-cdp; revenue/pipeline measurement ↔ Officer 6;
   data-governance compliance ↔ Inspector.
