# Skill — B2B 95-5 Future-Memory Building

In B2B, most buyers are not buying today. The **95-5 rule** (Ehrenberg-Bass / LinkedIn B2B
Institute): at any moment only **~5%** of potential buyers are **in-market**; the other ~95%
will buy *later* (long, infrequent purchase cycles). So advertising's main job is to **build
and refresh memory links** now, so the brand is *recalled* when a buyer enters the market —
not to convert the out-market 95% today.

> **Doctrine — and the critical caveat.** 95-5 describes **buyer TIMING**, not a budget split.
> **NEVER say "B2B budget should be ~95:5 brand:activation" — that conflation is REFUTED.** The
> empirically-discussed B2B brand:activation balance is **~50/50 (≈46:54)**, set via ESOV (the
> esov-sov soldier). 95-5 justifies *sustained brand-building for future buyers*; it does not
> set the number. Reuse Officer 1's **Category Entry Points** (the buying situations to be
> remembered in) — don't re-derive them. **Never invent** an in-market %, recall lift, or
> cycle-length figure — source it or label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown".

## Procedure
1. **Confirm the B2B context & cycle:** purchase frequency / cycle length, buying-group size,
   and the share in-market now vs later (cite the 95-5 rule; use category data where it exists,
   else label the ~5% as the rule's general estimate).
2. **Pull the B2B Category Entry Points** (from `category-entry-points`): the situational
   triggers a future buyer will recall from (e.g. "when our contract renews", "when we
   outgrow the current tool", "when a compliance deadline hits"). Map them to the buying group.
3. **Design future-memory building:** for each priority CEP, the memory you want established
   *before* the buyer is in-market — message, distinctive asset cue (link to mental-
   availability), and channel that reaches out-market buyers (broad reach, not just intent
   capture). Emphasise consistency and repetition over the long cycle.
4. **Balance 5% and 95%:** explicitly split the *role* (not the budget by 95:5) — what captures
   the in-market 5% (handed to demand/activation, O4: lead capture, high-intent search) vs what
   builds memories for the 95% (this brand-building work). State how they coexist.
5. **Reach for the 95%:** prioritise reaching ALL category buyers (penetration logic), since you
   can't know who will be in-market next; tie weight to ESOV (esov-sov) and note MMM calibration.
6. **Hand off:** future-memory plan → mental-availability (asset cues) + esov-sov (reach/weight);
   in-market 5% capture → Officer 4 (demand); the brand:activation *split* → Officer 6
   (budget-balance); tracking → Officer 6. Flag gaps for the Inspector.
