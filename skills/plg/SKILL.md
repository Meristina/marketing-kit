# Skill — Product-Led Growth (PLG)

Make the **product** the main engine of growth: users find value by *using* it, not by sitting
through a sales pitch. Plan the self-serve motion — how users discover, activate, retain, and
expand — and where revenue is captured.

> **Doctrine — fit first, then design.** PLG is powerful but **not universal**: it fits products
> with fast time-to-value, low-friction self-serve adoption, and a natural single-user or
> team-viral entry; it fits poorly where value needs heavy onboarding, procurement, or
> integration. **State the fit honestly**; many B2B motions are **hybrid** (PLG + sales-assisted
> for expansion). The **activation "aha" moment** is the heart — define it from real usage data,
> not a guess. **Never invent** an activation rate, conversion %, or benchmark — measure/source
> it or label `[assumption — verify]`; unknown → "unknown". PLG reuses the JTBD/value prop and
> hands paid-assist to full-funnel and lead handoff to lead-scoring.

## Procedure
1. **Fit assessment (gate):** is the product PLG-suited? Score on time-to-value, self-serve
   adoptability, single-user/team value, and viral/expansion potential. If weak, recommend a
   **hybrid or sales-led** motion instead — don't force PLG. State the verdict up front.
2. **Define the activation "aha" moment:** the specific action(s) where a new user first
   experiences core value (tie to the JTBD). Quantify it from usage data where it exists; this
   is the metric everything optimises toward.
3. **Map the self-serve funnel:** acquisition → signup → onboarding → **activation (aha)** →
   habit/retention → conversion (free→paid) → expansion. Quantify drop-off at each (reuse CRO
   discipline); identify the biggest friction to first value.
4. **Choose the model & monetisation:** free trial vs freemium vs free tool / reverse trial;
   the **value metric** (what you charge by — seats, usage, outcomes) and the **fence** (what's
   free vs paid) so free demonstrates value without cannibalising paid. Note the expansion path
   (NRR logic — ties to O5 retention).
5. **Onboarding & time-to-value:** the shortest path to the aha (remove setup friction, guide,
   templates, empty-state design); instrument it.
6. **Growth loops:** the self-reinforcing loops — product virality (invites/collaboration),
   content/SEO-led, network effects, paid-assisted — and which compound. Distinguish a loop
   from a one-off funnel.
7. **PLG metrics & guardrails:** activation rate, time-to-value, free→paid conversion, NRR,
   product-qualified leads (PQLs); guard against free cannibalising paid or vanity signups.
8. **Hand off:** paid-assist → full-funnel-paid-media; onboarding-conversion experiments →
   conversion-cro; PQL handoff → lead-scoring-lifecycle; expansion/retention → Officer 5;
   causal lift → O6.
